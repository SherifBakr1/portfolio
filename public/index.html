M.Sc. Mechanical Engineering Thesis — Minnesota State University, Mankato

Completed Jul 2019

Thesis: Development of a Sensing System for Underground Optic Fiber Cable Conduit Mapping

  • Problem Motivation: Protect high-value optic fiber cables from installation damage by mapping unknown underground conduit layouts in 3D}.
  • Algorithm Development: Created a novel algorithm for 3D position tracking using inertial sensors (IMU) and encoders, integrating Euler angle and quaternion methods for orientation.
  • Sensor Fusion: Designed a fusion pipeline to correct sensor drift, combining gyroscope and accelerometer data with refinement methods.
  • Experimental Validation: Built a physical test-bed with an IMU, duct rodder, and fiber blower; validated on three conduit layouts with measured mean errors of 0.39 m, −0.12 m, and −0.15 m respectively.
  • Impact: Delivered a working prototype sensing system capable of reconstructing 3D conduit maps, supporting fiber installation efficiency and safety for industry partner Condux International.
